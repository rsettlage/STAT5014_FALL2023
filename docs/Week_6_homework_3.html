Problem 3

In these exercises, you will import, munge, clean and summarize datasets from Wu and Hamada’s Experiments: Planning, Design and Analysis book used in the graduate experiment design class. For each dataset, please weave your code and text to describe both your process and observations. Make sure you create a tidy dataset describing the variables, create a summary table of the data, note any issues with the data, and create a single summary table of the data (kable(head(data),caption="XXX data ...")) and a single plot of each dataset highlighting some (ANY – your choice) feature of the data. For full credit, you will use the dplyr and tidyr packages for munging and summarizing the data for at least one function of the tidying. I do read your code submissions. Remember to label your plots including axis and any necessary legends.

The first two are warm-ups:

Part A

Ten parts were selected randomly from a line and duplicate measurements of the part’s wall thickness were taken by each of three operators of the measurement apparatus. Click on the link and you will see the data in your browser.
https://www2.isye.gatech.edu/~jeffwu/wuhamadabook/data/ThicknessGauge.dat

Part B

Brain weight (g) and body weight (kg) for 62 species.
https://www2.isye.gatech.edu/~jeffwu/wuhamadabook/data/BrainandBodyWeight.dat

What do you do when the rows are not uniform in data content?

Part C

Gold Medal performance for Olympic Men’s Long Jump, year is coded as 1900=0. Goodness, ragged arrays. Check out fread in the data.table package.
https://www2.isye.gatech.edu/~jeffwu/wuhamadabook/data/LongJumpData.dat

Using fread, you can specify more than one field separator and a skip value…

Part D

Triplicate measurements of tomato yield for two varieties of tomatos at three planting densities.
https://www2.isye.gatech.edu/~jeffwu/wuhamadabook/data/tomato.dat

Part E

Larvae counts at two ages given 5 different treatments in 8 blocks.
https://www2.isye.gatech.edu/~jeffwu/wuhamadabook/data/LarvaeControl.dat
